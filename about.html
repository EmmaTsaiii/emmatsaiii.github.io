Hi, I'm Emma

UX Designer | UI Designer | Interactive Designer

Currently, I am in my final year of studying Master of Interactive Design and Electronic Art in University of Sydney. We have recently collaborated with the Lake Macquarie City Council in teams of four to complete the design of interactive installations and interfaces for their new, high-tech Multi Arts Pavillion / Mima precinct. Applying the human-centred approach with the specific inflection of urban interaction, we were able to produce the corresponded interactive interfaces with our design solutions and tested via digital urban prototype.


As a UX designer, I try my best to make sure the design decisions are results of a human-centered process.

Transitioning from Food Science to UX Design was easier that I imagined. The way of thinking in UX design involves the steps used and learned when studying and working as a food product developer. Although I had to use new tools, the underlying concept of both fields ranging from research, development to implementation are fundamentally the same. It is essentially a transition in canvas – from food to digital interfaces.

Skills

• Design tools: Balsamiq, Figma, Adobe XD, Photoshop

• Design skills: User Interviews, Research, Persona Development, Brainstorming, Brainwriting, Storyboarding, Flow Chart, User Journey, Heuristic Evaluation, User Testing

• Development: JavaScript, basic knowledge of html/css

However, I realized the continuing as a food product developer provides me little opportunity to engage directly with consumers/users. Engaging with users during the design process to gather the interactive feedback is a core element in achieving a thoroughly designed product. User Experience Design is something that I really enjoy and am ready to start as a lifetime career.I am always ready for new projects and opportunities to accumulate my experience.
